Manufacturing

Phase 1: Digitizing the design


1. Prepare your artwork


Select a high-quality image. Start with a high-resolution image, such as a vector file (.AI, .EPS, .SVG), if possible, as it can be resized without losing quality. A high-resolution raster file (.JPEG, .PNG) can also work, but tracing it during the digitizing process will be more difficult. Simplify complex artwork. Intricate details and tiny text do not translate well into thread. Simplify fine lines, remove excessive small elements, and use bold, legible fonts. Gradients and soft shading are also not possible with standard embroidery and should be converted to solid colors. Choose thread colors and consider fabric type. Select the thread colors that match your design. Also, think about the fabric the design will be sewn on, as its texture and weight will influence your digitizing decisions.
